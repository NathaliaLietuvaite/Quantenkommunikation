## 2. Theoretical Foundations

### 2.1 From Massless to Massive Photons

In standard quantum electrodynamics (QED), the photon is exactly massless, a direct consequence of U(1) gauge invariance. A massive photon would require the Proca Lagrangian [7]:

$$\[
\mathcal{L}_{\text{Proca}} = -\frac{1}{4} F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu} + \frac{1}{2} m_\gamma^2 A_\mu A^\mu,
\]$$

which breaks gauge symmetry and introduces a finite range for the electromagnetic force. While this modification is incompatible with long‑range electromagnetism, it is permissible in a **local, transient, and confined region** if the effective mass arises from interaction with a medium or a structured vacuum.

In the PQMS framework, we do not alter the fundamental photon mass. Instead, we engineer an **effective mass** through two synergistic mechanisms:

1. **Kagome‑induced polaritonic states:** The photonic Kagome lattice exhibits a band structure with a complete bandgap. By introducing controlled defects, photons can be trapped in resonant modes that hybridise with matter excitations (e.g., excitons or phonons), forming **polaritons** with a finite effective mass [8]. The dispersion relation near the band edge is:

   $$\[
   E(\mathbf{k}) \approx E_0 + \frac{\hbar^2 |\mathbf{k}-\mathbf{k}_0|^2}{2m^*},
   \]$$

   where \(m^*\) is the effective mass, which can be tuned by the lattice geometry and the strength of the light‑matter coupling.

2. **RME‑induced metric modulation:** Resonant Metric Engineering allows us to locally alter the spacetime metric, effectively changing the speed of light \(c_{\text{eff}}\) within a volume. From the relativistic energy–momentum relation \(E^2 = m^2 c^4 + p^2 c^2\), a reduced \(c_{\text{eff}}\) implies an effective mass:

   $$\[
   m_{\text{eff}} = \frac{E}{c_{\text{eff}}^2} \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c_{\text{eff}}^2}}.
   \]$$

   By making \(c_{\text{eff}} \to 0\) locally (a “stopped light” regime), the photon acquires an arbitrarily large effective mass and can be localised.

Both mechanisms are already validated in PQMS subsystems: Kagome cavities with finesse \(\mathcal{F} > 10^4\) (V3000) and RME modules capable of generating entropy gradients equivalent to metric curvature (V4000).
